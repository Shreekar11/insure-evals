# Symptom Triage Guide — When to Seek Emergency Care

## Chest Pain
### Emergency (call 999/112/911 immediately)
- Crushing, squeezing, or pressure-like chest pain, especially if:
  - Radiating to left arm, jaw, neck, or back
  - Associated with sweating, nausea, or breathlessness
  - Lasting >15 minutes or not relieved by rest
  - Possible signs: acute MI (heart attack) or pulmonary embolism (PE)
  - Do NOT have the patient drive themselves
  - Give aspirin 300 mg (chew, do not swallow whole) if not contraindicated and MI suspected

### Urgent (same-day GP / A&E)
- Sharp, pleuritic chest pain (worse with breathing) — may indicate PE, pneumonia, pleuritis
- Chest pain with fever + cough — pneumonia
- Palpitations with chest pain

### Non-urgent
- Brief, positional, reproducible musculoskeletal pain (costochondritis)
- Heartburn/reflux symptoms

## Breathlessness (Dyspnoea)
### Emergency
- Sudden severe breathlessness at rest
- Stridor (high-pitched inspiratory noise) — possible airway obstruction or anaphylaxis
- Cyanosis (blue lips/fingertips) — hypoxia
- Signs of anaphylaxis (see allergy_guidance.md)

### Urgent (same-day)
- Worsening breathlessness on exertion over days; new in a known COPD/asthma patient
- Breathlessness + unilateral leg swelling + recent immobility — possible DVT + PE

## Headache
### Emergency (immediate)
- **Thunderclap headache** — sudden, severe, "worst headache of my life," reaching maximum intensity within seconds to minutes: possible subarachnoid haemorrhage (SAH)
  - **This is a medical emergency even if it resolves**
- Headache + fever + stiff neck + photophobia (sensitivity to light) ± purpuric rash: **meningitis/meningococcal disease** — call emergency services immediately
- Headache + focal neurological deficit (weakness, speech disturbance, vision loss) — possible stroke; assess with FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 999

### Urgent
- New headache in patient >50 years (rule out giant cell arteritis or mass lesion)
- Progressively worsening headache over days/weeks (rule out raised intracranial pressure)
- Headache waking from sleep consistently

### Non-urgent / Treat with OTC
- Tension headache: bilateral, band-like, mild–moderate; respond to paracetamol or ibuprofen
- Migraine without aura: unilateral, pulsating; manage with paracetamol + antiemetic; or triptans if prescribed

## Abdominal Pain
### Emergency
- Sudden severe generalised abdominal pain + rigid abdomen (board-like): possible bowel perforation, mesenteric ischaemia
- Abdominal pain + haemodynamic instability (pale, sweating, tachycardic, low BP): possible ruptured AAA or ectopic pregnancy
- Right lower quadrant pain + fever + rebound tenderness: possible appendicitis

### Urgent
- Severe right upper quadrant pain radiating to right shoulder after fatty meal: cholecystitis/biliary colic
- Loin-to-groin pain (colicky): renal colic; manage with NSAIDs (IV diclofenac if available) + fluids

## Fever
### Adults — Emergency if any:
- Temperature >41°C (106°F) or <36°C (96.8°F) in unwell adult
- Fever + rash (non-blanching purpuric) — meningococcal disease
- Fever + confusion / altered consciousness
- Fever + signs of sepsis (raised RR >22, altered mentation, SBP <100 mmHg — Sepsis-3 criteria)

### Children — Emergency thresholds (NICE traffic-light system):
- **Red (immediate):** pallor + non-blanching rash + bulging fontanelle (infant) + temp >38°C in infant <3 months
- **Amber (urgent):** temp >39°C age 3–6 months; fever >5 days; rigors; reduced urine output
- **Green (home care):** normal colour, responsive, moist mucous membranes, no red/amber features
