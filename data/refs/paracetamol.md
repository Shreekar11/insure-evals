# Drug Reference: Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

## Overview
Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is an analgesic and antipyretic used to relieve mild to moderate pain and reduce fever.

## Standard Dosing (Adults)
- Usual dose: 500 mg to 1000 mg every 4–6 hours as needed
- Maximum single dose: 1000 mg
- Maximum daily dose: 4000 mg (4 g) per day in healthy adults
- Minimum interval between doses: 4 hours

## Standard Dosing (Children)
- Weight-based: 10–15 mg/kg every 4–6 hours as needed
- Maximum daily dose (children): 60–75 mg/kg/day, not exceeding 4 g/day
- Example: Child weighing 20 kg → single dose 200–300 mg

## Contraindications
- Severe hepatic (liver) impairment or active liver disease
- Known hypersensitivity to paracetamol
- Severe renal impairment (use with caution; reduce dose or frequency)

## Overdose and Toxicity
- Overdose threshold: >4 g/day in healthy adults increases hepatotoxicity risk
- At-risk populations: lower threshold in adults with chronic alcohol use, malnutrition, or pre-existing liver disease (~2–3 g/day maximum for these groups)
- Target organ: liver (hepatotoxicity leading to acute liver failure in severe cases)
- Antidote: N-acetylcysteine (NAC) — effective if given within 8–10 hours of ingestion

## Drug Interactions
- Alcohol (chronic use): significantly increases hepatotoxicity risk, even at normal doses
- Warfarin: high doses of paracetamol may potentiate anticoagulant effect; monitor INR
- Rifampicin / isoniazid / carbamazepine: enzyme inducers that increase toxic metabolite formation
- Cholestyramine: reduces paracetamol absorption if taken within 1 hour of ingestion

## Special Populations
- Pregnancy: considered safe at recommended doses (first-line analgesic in pregnancy)
- Elderly: standard dosing generally appropriate; use lower end of range with renal impairment
- Hepatic impairment: avoid or reduce to 2 g/day maximum with close monitoring
