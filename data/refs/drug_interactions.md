# Drug Interactions Reference — Common High-Risk Pairs

## 1. Warfarin + NSAIDs (e.g., Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Aspirin)
- Interaction type: Pharmacodynamic (additive bleeding risk) + pharmacokinetic (CYP2C9 inhibition by some NSAIDs)
- Risk: Significantly increased bleeding risk; upper GI hemorrhage
- Management: Avoid combination; if unavoidable, monitor INR closely and use lowest effective NSAID dose for shortest duration
- Alternative analgesic: Paracetamol (preferred, but monitor INR with doses >2 g/day)

## 2. ACE Inhibitors + Potassium-Sparing Diuretics (e.g., Spironolactone)
- Interaction type: Pharmacodynamic (additive hyperkalemia)
- Risk: Dangerous hyperkalemia (high blood potassium), potentially life-threatening cardiac arrhythmia
- Management: Monitor serum potassium levels regularly; reduce doses; avoid in patients with renal impairment
- Examples: Lisinopril + spironolactone, ramipril + amiloride

## 3. SSRIs + Tramadol
- Interaction type: Pharmacodynamic (serotonin syndrome risk) + pharmacokinetic (CYP2D6)
- Risk: Serotonin syndrome — agitation, hyperthermia, tachycardia, clonus; potentially fatal
- Management: Avoid combination; if used, monitor closely; start at low doses; have cyproheptadine available
- Examples: Fluoxetine + tramadol (fluoxetine also inhibits CYP2D6, reducing tramadol activation)

## 4. Methotrexate + NSAIDs
- Interaction type: Pharmacokinetic (reduced renal clearance of methotrexate)
- Risk: Methotrexate toxicity — bone marrow suppression, mucositis, nephrotoxicity
- Management: Avoid NSAIDs in patients on methotrexate; use paracetamol; monitor FBC and renal function

## 5. Digoxin + Amiodarone
- Interaction type: Pharmacokinetic (amiodarone inhibits P-glycoprotein, increases digoxin levels)
- Risk: Digoxin toxicity — nausea, bradycardia, arrhythmia, visual disturbances
- Management: Reduce digoxin dose by 50% when starting amiodarone; monitor digoxin levels and ECG

## 6. Statins + Strong CYP3A4 Inhibitors (e.g., Clarithromycin, Itraconazole)
- Interaction type: Pharmacokinetic (increased statin plasma levels)
- Risk: Myopathy and rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown leading to acute kidney injury)
- Affected statins: Simvastatin, lovastatin (highest risk); atorvastatin (moderate risk)
- Management: Temporarily hold statin during short antibiotic courses; switch to pravastatin or rosuvastatin (lower interaction risk)

## 7. MAO Inhibitors + Sympathomimetics / Opioids
- Interaction type: Pharmacodynamic (serotonergic / adrenergic crisis)
- Risk: Hypertensive crisis (with sympathomimetics) or serotonin syndrome (with pethidine/meperidine)
- Management: 14-day washout after stopping MAOI before starting these drugs; use morphine cautiously
- Note: Pethidine (meperidine) is absolutely contraindicated with MAOIs
