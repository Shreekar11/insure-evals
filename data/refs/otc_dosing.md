# OTC Medication Dosing Reference

## Paracetamol (see also paracetamol.md for full detail)
- Adults: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours; max 4000 mg/day
- Children 6–12 years: 250–500 mg every 4–6 hours; max 4 doses/24h
- Children 2–5 years: 120–250 mg every 4–6 hours
- Infants 3 months–2 years: 60–120 mg every 4–6 hours
- Do not give to infants under 2 months without medical advice

## Ibuprofen (NSAID)
- Adults: 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours; max 1200 mg/day OTC (up to 2400 mg/day under medical supervision)
- Children >6 months: 5–10 mg/kg every 6–8 hours; max 40 mg/kg/day
- Contraindications: active GI ulceration or bleeding; severe renal/hepatic/cardiac impairment; third trimester of pregnancy; aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease
- Take with food to reduce GI irritation

## Aspirin (Acetylsalicylic Acid)
- Analgesic/antipyretic (adults only): 300–900 mg every 4–6 hours; max 4000 mg/day
- Antiplatelet (low-dose): 75–100 mg once daily
- **NOT for use in children <16 years** — risk of Reye's syndrome (potentially fatal hepatic and neurological complication)
- Contraindications: active peptic ulcer, haemophilia, children <16 years

## Antihistamines (for allergy / hay fever)
### Non-sedating (preferred for daytime use)
- Loratadine 10 mg: once daily (adult and children >6 years)
- Cetirizine 10 mg: once daily (adult and children >6 years); may cause mild sedation in some
- Fexofenadine 120 mg: once daily for allergic rhinitis; 180 mg daily for urticaria

### Sedating
- Chlorphenamine (chlorpheniramine) 4 mg: every 4–6 hours; max 24 mg/day; caution driving
- Diphenhydramine 25–50 mg: every 4–6 hours; also used as a sleep aid (avoid in elderly — anticholinergic risk)

## Antacids / Acid Suppressants
- Calcium carbonate (antacid): 500–1000 mg as needed for heartburn; do not exceed 7500 mg/day
- Omeprazole (PPI, OTC): 20 mg once daily before breakfast for max 14 days for heartburn; if >14 days needed, seek medical advice
- Ranitidine: withdrawn from most markets due to NDMA contamination concerns

## Decongestants
- Pseudoephedrine (oral): 60 mg every 4–6 hours; max 240 mg/day; OTC in many markets (often behind-the-counter)
- Oxymetazoline (nasal spray): 2–3 sprays per nostril twice daily; **max 3 days** (longer use causes rebound congestion — rhinitis medicamentosa)
- Contraindications (pseudoephedrine): uncontrolled hypertension, MAO inhibitor use (within 14 days), severe coronary artery disease

## Loperamide (anti-diarrhoeal)
- Adults: 4 mg initial dose, then 2 mg after each loose stool; max 16 mg/day OTC
- Not for children under 12 without medical advice; not for bloody diarrhoea or acute dysentery
- Warning: doses above recommended can cause serious cardiac arrhythmia
