# Allergy and Hypersensitivity Guidance

## Penicillin Allergy

### Cross-Reactivity
- Penicillin + other penicillins: 100% cross-reactivity (all penicillins share the beta-lactam + thiazolidine ring)
- Penicillin + cephalosporins: ~1–2% cross-reactivity (lower than historically believed; shared R1 side chains matter more than the beta-lactam)
- Penicillin + carbapenems: ~1% cross-reactivity
- Penicillin + monobactams (aztreonam): no significant cross-reactivity, EXCEPT aztreonam shares R1 chain with ceftazidime

### Management of Penicillin Allergy
- Mild/unknown reaction (rash, nausea): re-challenge or cross-react with cephalosporin is often safe — assess risk
- Anaphylaxis history: avoid all penicillins and related beta-lactams; consider skin testing before cephalosporin use
- Alternative antibiotics for common infections: macrolides (azithromycin), clindamycin, vancomycin (MRSA), doxycycline

## Aspirin / NSAID Hypersensitivity

### Types
1. **Aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease (AERD):** bronchospasm + nasal polyposis. Mechanism: COX-1 inhibition shifts arachidonic acid to leukotriene pathway
2. **Aspirin-induced urticaria/angioedema:** typically cross-reacts with all NSAIDs
3. **Single-drug allergy:** true immunological allergy to specific NSAID; may tolerate others

### Management
- AERD patients: avoid all NSAIDs; use paracetamol (safe at doses ≤1 g), selective COX-2 inhibitors (celecoxib) with caution
- Aspirin urticaria: avoid all NSAIDs; paracetamol usually safe at low doses (500 mg)

## Contrast Media Allergy
- Iodinated contrast media (ICM): risk of allergy-like reactions in ~0.5–3% of patients
- Pre-medication regimen (for prior reaction): prednisolone 50 mg oral 13h, 7h, and 1h before; chlorphenamine 10 mg IV 1h before
- Gadolinium (MRI contrast): lower reaction rate; note nephrogenic systemic fibrosis risk in severe renal impairment (eGFR <30 mL/min)

## Latex Allergy
- Risk groups: healthcare workers, patients with spina bifida, patients with >5 surgical procedures
- Cross-reactive foods (latex-fruit syndrome): banana, avocado, kiwi, chestnut
- Management: latex-free environment for all procedures; alternative equipment mandatory

## Anaphylaxis Management (First-Line)
1. **Adrenaline (epinephrine) IM 0.5 mg (1:1000) into anterolateral thigh** — this is the FIRST and most important intervention
2. Call for emergency assistance
3. Position supine with legs elevated (or left lateral if unconscious/vomiting)
4. Oxygen + IV access
5. IV fluids (crystalloid) for hypotension
6. Antihistamines and corticosteroids are ADJUNCTS only — do NOT give before or instead of adrenaline
- Adrenaline dose in children: 0.01 mg/kg IM, max 0.5 mg
